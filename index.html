TRACKS

TRACK 1

Plant disease detection

Develop a machine learning model that can accurately identify and segment regions of plant leaves affected by disease/pest in images provided in a dataset. The model should be able to classify the images into different disease categories and highlight the affected areas on the leaves. The participants will be judged based on the accuracy, inference time and novelty of the architecture used.

TRACK 2

Integrating mobile applications and cloud based expert systems

Develop an Android mobile application that can interact with a provided REST API for plant disease detection. The app should be able to send an image of a plant leaf to the API, receive the disease classification, disease stage, and other metadata in response, and display the results in a user-friendly interface. The participants will be judged on the app's User Interface, User Experience and functionality of the API integration.

TRACK 3

Path planning (RGB data, depth data)

Develop a computer vision algorithm/machine learning model that can accurately identify drivable areas in an unlabelled dataset of RGB and depth data from a sugar cane field. The data is taken from a restricted area where robots are used to move around. The algorithm should be able to distinguish the drivable areas from non-drivable areas in the field. The participants will be evaluated based on the accuracy of the algorithm and its ability to generalize to new data.

FAQs

How does it work?

Individuals/Team of maximum 3 participants can apply for the hackathon and choose a track they are interested to work in. The selected Individuals will be invited for the hackathon in-person at Vellore Institute of Technology, Vellore. Specific Problem statements will be provided after the commencement of the hackathon and teams will have 36 hours to implement their ideas.

What is the deadline to register?

The last date to register for the hackathon is 20th February 2023.

What is the participation fee?

This event is completely free for VITians and Non-VITians.

Will food be provided?

Food will be provided to Non-VIT participant and refereshments will be provided to all participants.

Who can participate in this hackathon?

All under and post graduate students irrespective of branch and year, who are registered under affiliated institutes across India. Reimbursements for any expenses will not be provided.

What is the required team size?

The team can consist of 2 to 3 members. Individuals applying for the hackathon will be paired in teams by the organising committee.

What should I have?

All the participants are required to have laptops for the hack. Each teams participating in the AI and Robotics track wil be provided Nvidia Jetson Nano Boards along with camera and WiFi modules (to be used during the hackathon). The teams participating in the robotics track will be given access to a Ground Robot Platform (shared between all the teams).
